# Anatomical Language

## Prelab Activity 1.1

### Directional terms
- **Anterior (or ventral):** toward the front of the body.
- **Posterior (or dorsal):** toward the back of the body.
- **Superior (or cranial):** toward the head; above.
- **Inferior (or caudal):** away from the head; below.
- **Lateral:** away from the midline of the body.
- **Medial:** toward the midline of the body.
- **Proximal:** closer to the point of attachment or trunk.
- **Distal:** farther from the point of attachment or trunk.
- **Superficial:** toward or at the body surface.
- **Deep:** away from the body surface; more internal.

### Body cavities
- **Cranial:** cavity within the skull that contains the brain.
- **Abdominal:** cavity below the diaphragm that contains many digestive organs.
- **Pericardial:** cavity surrounding the heart.
- **Pleural:** cavities surrounding the lungs.
- **Ventral:** large anterior body cavity that includes the thoracic and abdominopelvic cavities.

### Body planes
- **Sagittal plane:** divides the body into right and left portions.
- **Midsagittal plane:** divides the body into equal right and left halves.
- **Parasagittal plane:** divides the body into unequal right and left portions.
- **Frontal plane:** divides the body into anterior and posterior portions.
- **Transverse plane:** divides the body into superior and inferior portions.
- **Oblique plane:** divides the body at an angle.

## Lab Activity 1.4
1. The elbow is located **proximal** to the wrist.
2. The umbilicus is located **inferior** to the sternum.
3. The nose is located **medial** to the ears.
4. The mouth is located **superior** to the chin.

## Lab Activity 1.5
1. **From lying face-up on the ground with knees bent:**
   - Extend the knees.
   - Bring the feet under the body.
   - Raise the trunk to an upright standing position.
   - Place the upper limbs at the sides.
   - Supinate the forearms so the palms face anteriorly.
   - Face the head and eyes anteriorly.

2. **From a seated position on the floor with legs straight and arms folded across the chest:**
   - Uncross the upper limbs and place the arms at the sides.
   - Flex the hips and knees as needed to bring the body upward.
   - Stand upright.
   - Extend the knees fully.
   - Position the feet flat and pointing forward.
   - Supinate the forearms so the palms face anteriorly.

## Post Lab Activity 1.1

### Systems
- The **integumentary** system forms the external body covering.
- The **integumentary** system protects deeper tissues from injury.
- The **integumentary** system synthesizes vitamin D.
- Organs of the **endocrine** system secrete chemicals called hormones into the blood.
- The **respiratory** system keeps blood supplied with oxygen and disposes of unwanted carbon dioxide.
- The **reproductive** system produces sperm or eggs and sex hormones.

### Homeostasis
- In a **positive** feedback system, a change in a condition is sensed and amplified.
- In a **negative** feedback system, a change in a condition is sensed and returned toward its previous level.

- The thumbs are **lateral** to the pinky fingers.

- The chest is **anterior** to the shoulder blades.

- The genitals are **medial** to the hips.
- The ankles are **distal** to the shins.

- The lips are **inferior** and **medial** to the ears.

### Regional terms review
- "Nasal" refers to the **nose**.
- "Oral" refers to the **mouth**.
- "Cervical" refers to the **neck**.
- "Acromial" refers to the **point of the shoulder**.
- "Axillary" refers to the **armpit**.
- "Abdominal" refers to the **abdomen**.
- "Brachial" refers to the **arm**.
- "Antecubital" refers to the **front of the elbow**.
- "Antebrachial" refers to the **forearm**.
- "Pelvic" refers to the **pelvis**.

### Planes
- The **frontal** or **coronal** plane separates the anterior and posterior portions of an object.
- The **transverse** or **horizontal** plane separates the superior and inferior portions of an object.

### Cavities
- The cranial cavity is within the **dorsal** cavity.
- The spinal or vertebral cavity is within the **dorsal** cavity.
- The thoracic cavity is within the **ventral** cavity.
- The abdominopelvic cavity is within the **ventral** cavity.
- The brain is found in the **cranial** cavity.
- The spinal cord is found in the **vertebral (spinal)** cavity.
